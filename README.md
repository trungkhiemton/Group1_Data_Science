# ABSTRACT

Credit card fraud has become a major financial issue, causing billions of dollars in losses annually. Machine learning techniques can help identify fraudulent activities by detecting unusual transaction patterns and behaviors. Credit card fraud may occur due to stolen physical cards or unauthorized access to confidential card information. Various machine learning algorithms are commonly applied for fraud detection tasks. This project aims to build a machine-learning-based system for detecting credit card fraud. The proposed model will be trained using historical transaction data and tested on a separate dataset containing previously unseen transactions to evaluate its performance.

Keywords: Credit Card Fraud Detection, Fraudulent Activity, Financial Fraud, K-Nearest Neighbors, Random Forest Classifiers



# Overview
The increasing use of credit cards in everyday transactions has made security and customer protection a major concern for financial institutions. According to credit card statistics from 2021, approximately 2.8 billion people worldwide used credit cards in 2019, with many users owning at least one card. At the same time, incidents of credit card fraud have continued to rise significantly. Reports of credit card fraud in the United States increased sharply in 2020.

There are mainly two forms of credit card fraud. The first occurs when criminals open a new credit card account using someone else’s identity, while the second involves unauthorized use of an existing credit card account by stealing card information. The growing number of fraud cases over recent years has attracted significant attention and highlighted the urgent need for effective fraud detection systems. This situation motivated us to address the problem analytically by applying various machine learning techniques to identify fraudulent credit card transactions among large volumes of transaction data.



# Project Goals

The primary objective of this project is to detect fraudulent credit card transactions, as identifying such transactions is crucial to prevent customers from being charged for purchases they did not make. To achieve this goal, several machine learning techniques will be applied and evaluated for fraud detection.

Furthermore, the project aims to compare the performance and results of different machine learning models in order to determine the most effective and suitable approach for detecting fraudulent credit card transactions. The comparison will be supported by numerical evaluations and graphical representations of the results.

In addition, this project also reviews previous studies and existing techniques used in fraud detection to gain a deeper understanding of how fraudulent activities can be identified within large datasets.
